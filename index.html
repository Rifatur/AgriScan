Small, dark brown to black lesions that appear on older leaves first. These lesions often have a target-like appearance with concentric rings. As the disease progresses, leaves turn yellow and may drop prematurely.

Caused by the fungus Alternaria solani. The fungus thrives in warm, humid conditions and can survive in plant debris and soil. Spores are spread by wind, rain, and irrigation water.

  • Remove and destroy infected plant material
  • Apply fungicides containing chlorothalonil or copper
  • Ensure proper plant spacing for good air circulation
  • Water at the base of plants to keep foliage dry
  • Crop rotation and sanitation of garden tools
